The "Between-Run" economy is the engine that drives daily retention. If the Weekly Challenge is the "Final Exam," then the daily economy is the "Study and Prep" phase.

In keeping with the **Marathon (2026)** vibe, the player's home base should feel like a sterile, high-tech clean room that is slowly being "infected" by the data brought back from the dungeon. We’ll call this the **Neural Deck.**

---

## **1\. The Neural Deck (The Hub)**

The Hub is a minimalist browser interface.

* **Visuals:** A 3D or high-res 2D view of your **Shell** suspended in a maintenance rack.  
* **The Atmosphere:** No music, just the hum of a server room and the occasional "glitch" sound effect.  
* **Core Terminology:** We don't "Craft" or "Shop"; we **Compile** and **Initialize**.

---

## **2\. The Currency Ecosystem**

To balance the web-based economy, we use a three-tier currency system.

| Currency | Source | Use |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Raw Scrap** | Common drops; salvaged trash items. | Basic repairs and purchasing "Standard" Software. |
| **Neural Blueprints** | Rare drops from Tier 2/3 enemies. | Unlocks the ability to compile specific **Firmware**. |
| **Flux** | Extracted from deep runs or events. | The "Premium" non-paid currency. Used to upgrade Shell Port configurations. |

---

## **3\. The Core Loop: Scavenge → Compile → Burn**

### **Step 1: Scavenging (The Daily Run)**

Players enter "Neural Simulations." These are unlimited runs.

* **Goal:** Reach a **Stability Anchor** with as much **Software** and **Flux** as possible.  
* **Risk:** If you die, the Software is lost, but you keep 25% of the **Scrap** as a "pity" payout.

### **Step 2: Compiling (The Workshop)**

In the Neural Deck, players use **Blueprints** and **Flux** to create permanent "Recipes."

* *Example:* You find the `Phase_Shift.sh` Blueprint. You spend 500 Flux to "Compile" it. You can now equip this Firmware on any run, but if you lose a run, you have to spend a small amount of Scrap to "Re-Initialize" it.

### **Step 3: Burning (The Customization)**

Software is "Burned" onto equipment.

* Unlike Firmware (which is a choice), Software is a **consumable resource**.  
* If you have a favorite gun, you "Burn" `Vampire.exe` onto it. That gun is now powerful for **one run**. If you extract, the software stays. If you die, the software is wiped from the gun, and you must find/buy a new copy.

---

## **4\. The "One-Shot" Preparation (The Weekly Ritual)**

The economy builds toward the **Weekly Challenge**. Throughout the week, the player's goal is to amass a "God-Tier" loadout.

* **The Vault:** A small, protected inventory (e.g., 30 slots). Items in the Vault cannot be used in Daily Simulations. They are "Locked" for the Weekly Challenge.  
* **The Ritual:** On Sunday, players move their best Software and Firmware into their **Active Shell**.  
* **The Stakes:** In the Weekly Challenge, you aren't just losing Software; you are losing your **invested time**. If you fail the One-Shot, your Active Shell is "Factory Reset"—all Software is wiped, and all Flux upgrades are downgraded by 1 tier.

---

# Blueprint Reset

Resetting blueprints is a bold move that aligns perfectly with the **"Temporal Scarcity"** and **"Vibrant Decay"** themes. In a traditional roguelike, you start with nothing every run; in an extraction game, you build an empire. By resetting blueprints weekly, you are creating a **"Seasonal Extraction"** model that keeps the playing field level and the "Scavenge" loop eternally relevant.

---

### **1\. Analysis: Why the Weekly Reset Works**

* **Solves Power Creep:** It prevents veteran players from amassing an unbeatable "perfect" library of abilities that trivialize the game.  
* **Forces Meta-Shifts:** If the "Phase-Shift" blueprint is removed or becomes rare next week, players are forced to experiment with new synergies.  
* **Value of the "One Shot":** It makes the Weekly Challenge truly the "Final Form" of that week’s data. You are using the best of what *this specific week* provided.

---

### **2\. Data Entropy**

#### **The "Version Patch" Approach (Structured)**

When the week resets, the "Compiler" moves to a new version (e.g., v1.0 to v1.1).

* Old blueprints aren't deleted; they become **"Legacy Code."** \* Legacy Code is 50% less effective (longer cooldowns, less damage) than the "Current Patch" blueprints found in the new seed.  
* **Why this works:** Players still have their favorite toys, but they are incentivized to find the "Current" versions to remain competitive on the leaderboard.

---

### **4\. The Progression Cycle**

With the addition of Blueprint Resets, the game's loop is now a perfect "Ouroboros":

Example Flow:
1. **Monday:** The Purge occurs. A new seed and a new "Winner's Item" are revealed.
2. **Tue-Fri:** **Scavenge Phase.** Players run simulations to find this week's "Meta" blueprints and stock up on Software.  
3. **Saturday:** **The Optimization.** Players "Burn" their best Software onto their Shells and practice with their finalized Blueprint builds.  
4. **Sunday:** **The One-Shot.** The community watches as the top runners risk their weekly builds for the chance to become the next "Architect."
